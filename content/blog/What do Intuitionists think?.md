They think that we should not be able to go from something "not" being the case , to being something 'is" the case.
That is, $$\lnot \lnot A \not \implies A$$
>[!quote] Quote
>Just because you have ruled out something being false, it does not mean that it needs to be true.

Simply, it could just mean that we cannot say something about it either way.  

Another *loose* way to think about this is "information"
$\lnot \lnot A \not \implies A$
$\lnot \lnot A$ carries negative information about the world
$A$ carries positive information.

We cannot go from negative information to positive information.
Double negation DOES NOT imply NO negation.

We do not assume law of excluded middle , which says  $\lnot A \lor A$ is valid (is always true, irrespective of the assignment to A).

$$
\text{double negation elimination} \iff \text{assuming law of excluded middle}
$$
