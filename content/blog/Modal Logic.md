# Notes 
##### Topics/Keywords:
- Modal free formula : if it does not contain $\square$ or $\lozenge$ 
- Instance of a formula A: result of replacing all occurrences of a propositional variable A by some other formula. $A[D/p]$  is called a substitution instance of A.

##### Valid Formulas:
Formulas that are true in all models,i.e. true at every world in every Model
Regardless of how $\square$ and diamond are interpreted

>[!example] Example:
>$\square( p \wedge q) \rightarrow \square p$ is valid
>$\square p \rightarrow p$ not valid
>

##### Tautological Instances 
A modal free formula is a tautology if it is true under every truth assignment. 
Every tautology is true at every world in every model.

 >A modal formula B is a tautological instance if and only if there is a modal-free tautology A with propositional variables $p_1, . . . , p_n$ and formulas $D_1, . . . , D_n$ such that $B ≡ A[D_1/p_1, . . . ,D_n /p_n]$

Every tautological instance is valid.
