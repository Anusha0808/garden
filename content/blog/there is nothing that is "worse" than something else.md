We often think that doing $A$ is worse than doing $B$, where $A, B$ are some actions. For instance, *killing your friend* is worse than *lying to your friend*.
But is it?
Since we know both of them are usually considered bad (or for the sake of this post, let's agree both of these acts are bad) then , it means no person *should* do them? If a person does them, we have somehow arrived at a "false", We know in logic, all instances of false are same? Which means, if you have done A, which is bad, you might as well do B now since you are no longer a person.
