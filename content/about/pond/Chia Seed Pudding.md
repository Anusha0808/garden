## INGREDIENTS
- [ ] 1 cup plain yogurt
- [ ] 1 cup milk
- [ ] ¼ cup chia seeds
- [ ] 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, optional
- [ ] Sunflower, sesame and pumpkin Seeds, and nuts _(optional)_
- [ ] Unsweetened Cocoa Powder _(optional)_
- [ ] Bananas and other fruits _(optional)_ 

## INSTRUCTIONS
- Whisk together yogurt, milk, syrup, and vanilla extract in a medium-sized bowl.
- Add cocoa powder and chia seeds to yogurt mixture, wait for 5 minutes and give it another mix. We do this because chia seeds tend to stick together when mixed with liquid. Doing so helps break up any lingering clumps of chia seeds.
- Cover with stretch film, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
- When ready to eat, ladle into bowls and top each bowl with 1 banana cut into appropriately sized pieces, 1 teaspoon of pumpkin/sunflower seeds, 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds and nuts. Sprinkle some Cocoa powder again on top. If you have berries, add them!! 
- Eat while still cold.

## VARIATIONS & SUGGESTIONS
- You can substitute unsweetened cocoa powder with `matcha powder` or `protein powder`! I am yet to try out these variations but I am sure they will be very tasty ( and obviously healthy ) too! I am particularly curious how it will go with matcha powder because I am a chocolate fan!
- If you are a `coffee` addict like me, you can sprinkle some coffee as well on top while sprinkling cocoa powder.
- I looove `cinnamon`!! So I usually sprinkle a bit of cinnamon powder as well on top!
- Some people just ditch out the yogurt completely, and they use only milk as their base. I wanted to have some yogurt , because ~~why not~~ it makes the mixture thicker and yogurt has protein! If you choose to just make it out of milk, you might have to check out the consistency a bit, and I have seen people mostly making this out of `milk` and not `milk + yogurt`. 
- You can obviously sprinkle some corn flakes or museli on top to add that extra crunchiness
